Difference Between Born And Borne

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Thursday, September 19, 2024

The English language can be confusing, especially when it comes to words that sound the same but have different meanings. Born and borne are two such words. Born is an adjective and a past participle of the verb bear. On the other hand, borne is the past participle of the verb bear as well, but it is used differently. To understand the difference between born and borne, it is important to understand what each word means.

For example, one might say, “I was born in 1998.” It can also be used to describe a trait or characteristic that a person or animal has had since birth. For example, one might say, “He was born with blue eyes.”

Borne

Borne is the past participle of the verb bear. This means that it is used to describe an action that has been done. For example, one might say, “He has borne the burden of his family’s financial problems.” It can also be used to describe something that has been carried or transported from one place to another. For example, one might say, “The goods were borne from the factory to the warehouse.”
