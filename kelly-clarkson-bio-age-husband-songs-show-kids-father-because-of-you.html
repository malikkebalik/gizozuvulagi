Kelly Clarkson American Idol

Kelly Clarkson started her career when she auditioned for American Idol which was an Upcoming Talent show in May 2002.  The support of her friends and encouragement she got a golden ticket a pass to the Hollywood rounds and she was eventually won as the winner of the inaugural season of the show on September 4, 2002. Her successful stint earned her a million-dollar record deal with ‘RCA Records.’

Kelly Clarkson Before Your Love

She later started her music career on September 17, 2002, with the double -A-Side singles” Before Your Love” and A Moment Like This.’ The single became one of the best-selling singles of 2002 and both singles jumped a record 52 positions to top the ‘Billboard Hot 100’ chart.

Kelly Clarkson My December

She later released her third studio album “My December ” on June 22, 2007, which caused a Feud with her producer and music mogul, Clive Davis, who wanted her to record songs with more universal appeal. The album received positive reviews, its sale numbers brought to the lack of promotion.

Kelly Clarkson Stronger

Stronger is a song by Kelly Clarkson on January 17, 2012, the song served as its second single through RCA Records. Written by Jörgen Elofsson, Ali Tamposi, and David Gamson, with additional writing and production by Greg Kurstin, (Stronger) is an uptempo song which draws from the genres of dance-pop and electropop. Its lyrical content mainly explores themes of empowerment and recovery following a heartbreak, with the chorus inspired by a Friedrich Nietzsche quotation: “That which does not kill us makes us stronger.

Kelly Clarkson Breakaway

Breakaway is a song recorded by American singer Kelly Clarkson. The song, written by Matthew Gerrard, Bridget Benenate and Avril Lavigne, it was originally intended for Lavigne’s first studio album, Let Go (2002). After being deemed unsuitable for the album, it was passed to Clarkson to be recorded as a soundtrack for the film The Princess Diaries 2: Royal Engagement. Clarkson, who was finishing her second studio album at that time, recorded “Breakaway” to tide her fans over until the first single from her new album was released. This is one of her most successful albums. It received 6x platinum certification in the USA and was also certified multi-platinum in many other countries like Australia, England, Ireland, Canada, and New Zealand.

Kelly Clarkson Because Of You

Because of You is a song recorded by American pop singer Kelly Clarkson for her second studio album. It was written by Clarkson along with its producers David Hodges and Ben Moody, both from Evanescence. The song was released on August 16, 2005, by RCA Records, as the third single from Breakaway. Clarkson originally wrote “Because of You” when she was 16 years old to cope with the emotional distress caused by her parents’ divorce. She wanted the song to be included on her debut studio album, Thankful 2003, but her record label rejected the song. The thankful album “Thankful was a commercial success, selling over 4.5 million copies worldwide. It sold over 2.7 million copies in the USA and received 2x platinum certification.

Kelly Clarkson Piece By Piece

Piece by Piece is a song by American singer Kelly Clarkson taken from her seventh studio album of the same name. She wrote the track with producer Greg Kurstin. A midtempo pop song about restoring someone’s faith in love and family relationships, Clarkson promulgated Piece by Piece as a sequel to the song (Because of You) but with a “happy ending”. After a discussion with her sister about their family life, she introspectively wrote the song in a first-person narrative to her father, whose neglect of his family was used as a juxtaposition to her husband’s unconditional love for her and their daughter, whom they both promised to never abandon.

Kelly Clarkson Heartbeat Song

Heartbeat Song is a song by American singer Kelly Clarkson from her seventh studio album, Piece by Piece (2015). Written by Mitch Allan, Audra Mae, Kara DioGuardi, and Jason Evigan, the song is an uptempo synthpop track produced by Greg Kurstin. Lyrically, it sings of meeting a person who restores someone’s faith in love. Inspired by the 1980s synthpop music, “Heartbeat Song” was released by RCA Records as the album’s lead single on January 12, 2015.
