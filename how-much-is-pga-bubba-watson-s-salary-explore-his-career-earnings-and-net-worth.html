Bubba Watson's Career Earnings & Achievements

Bubba became a professional in 2003, and in his first season, he made over $1 million. Since then, he has won nearly $24 million in tournament prize money.

He got started when he signed up for the Nationwide Tour, where he competed until 2005. He was eligible for the PGA Tour the following year and had the longest drive on the circuit (319.6 yards). He performed admirably at the 2007 U.S. Open and tied for fifth place.

[image-2]

On June 27, 2010, Watson won his first PGA Tour title at the Travelers Championship in Cromwell, Connecticut, on the second hole of a sudden-death playoff. At the Whistling Straights PGA Championship, he came in second to Martin Kaymer. He won his second PGA Tour contest in January 2011.

In seven tournaments, Watson started 2012 with three top-five results. Later that year, Bubba won his first major championship at the Masters, propelling him to an astounding No. 4 on the world rankings.

He had some difficulties for the remainder of the 2012 campaign, missing the cut at the Memorial Tournament and the U.S. Open. After placing 14th at the Arnold Palmer Invitational to start the year, Watson returned to the Masters as the defending champion.
