Javon Tirus Ford Metropolitan Funeral

Posted by Martina Birk on Wednesday, August 28, 2024

Romans 8:38-39  “For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord.”

On Monday, March 27th, Ja’von Ti’rus Ford fell asleep, although unexpected, he passed but not without leaving a mark on this world. Even though Ja’von was taken too soon, he lived a life full of love, surrounded by people who adored him.

Ja’von was born April 30, 1993, in Beaufort, SC. He is the first-born son of Brevonda Carothers & Robert Ford. He was educated in the Western Branch public school systems. At the young age of 10, Ja’von fell in love with football. He excelled and dominated the football field, as a running back. Commonly known as JFord #34, Javon’s passion showed on and off the field.

Ja’von loved sports, especially football. He loved to dance, listen to music, and make those around him smile and feel comfortable. He had a heart of gold, and everyone knew this. Most importantly, Ja’von loved his family more than anything. He loved his children, parents, siblings, and friends known as the stonewinnerz. He was most known for telling jokes and giving the best hugs. Ja’von exemplified what it meant to live life in the moment.

Ja’von leaves to cherish his memories his children Aniyah Doss & Jasiah Ford (mother-Angel Doss), Kinlie Ford (mother-Karlie Lindgren), his sister Ruenette Hoffman (Greg), his nephew Myles, nieces Brevonda & Harper, his brother Ca’ron Ford, his sister-cousins Kiayana & Destiny Johnson, a plethora of aunts, uncles & cousins, and a host of friends.
