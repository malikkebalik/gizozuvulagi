Background and Career:

Melanie Martinez was born on April 28, 1995, in Astoria, Queens, and raised in Baldwin, New York, United States to parents Mery and Jose Martinez, who are of Dominican and Puerto Rican descent. Her family moved to Baldwin, New York, on Long Island when Martinez was four years old. She wrote poetry as a child and found solace in photography and painting. Motivated by diverse artists like Tim Burton and Amy Winehouse, her creative spirit flourished.

In 2012, she rose to stardom on season 3 of “The Voice,” charming audiences with her unique vocals and dark fairytale aesthetic. Despite being eliminated in the fifth week, she earned a loyal fanbase and signed with Atlantic Records. Her debut EP, “Dollhouse” (2014), featured the hit single “Carousel,” setting her signature style.

In 2015, her debut album, “Cry Baby,” cemented her status as a pop icon. She directed and starred in music videos for her songs, creating a cohesive visual universe that amplified the album’s influence. In 2019, she released her follow-up album, “K-12,” a concept album set within a fictional school. In 2020, she released the EP “After School,” offering a little lighter tone while retaining her signature style.
