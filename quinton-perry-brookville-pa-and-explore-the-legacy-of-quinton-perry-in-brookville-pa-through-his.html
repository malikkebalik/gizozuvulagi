Pennsylvania Teen Killed in Tragic Car Crash, Leaves Behind Legacy of Kindness

A beloved son, brother, and friend, 17-year-old Quinton Paul Perry of Brookville, Pennsylvania, passed away on December 16, 2023 as a result of a devastating car accident.

Described by loved ones as energetically positive and innately kind, Quinton brought light to the lives of the many people lucky enough to know him. Though his time on this earth was cut short, the imprint he left will remain eternally.

Promising Young Life Cut Short

A senior at Brookville Area Junior/Senior High School, Quinton had aspirations to pursue mechanics and auto work after graduation. He had already received an acceptance letter to Universal Technical Institute and was employed at Pizza Hut while finishing up high school.

Quinton took his faith seriously as a member of both the Indiana Seventh-day Adventist Church and the Brookville First Church of God. He approached all facets of life with positivity and resolve, extending goodwill to classmates, teammates, coworkers and community members alike.

The tragic accident severed a future brimming with promise for Quinton. But the sincere affection felt by those in his orbit speaks to a life lived fully and impactfully.

Leaves Legacy of Laughter and Compassion

With an energetic spirit and magnetic personality, Quinton had a knack for making others feel at ease. His ready smile and empathetic nature endeared him to peers and adults.

Sophomore Kayleigh Rhodes touchingly recalled:

“In the short time I knew Quinton and participated in track with him he was kind and caring to those around him and was always cracking jokes, he never failed to make anyone around him laugh.”

Even those not personally acquainted with Quinton recognized his standing as a universally-liked classmate. Though gone too soon, his mark as a funny, big-hearted friend persists.

Support Pours in from Community

As word spread of the accident that caused Quinton’s premature death, an outpouring of community support flooded in. Students and staff at DuBois Central Catholic High School organized a “blue day” to honor his memory. Brookville Area High School opened its doors the Sunday after Quinton’s passing to provide counseling resources.

On social media, fellow students expressed profound grief while celebrating Quinton’s spirited kindness. Wrote one friend: “I will miss that smile more than you know.”

Leaves Family Mourning His Absence

Quinton leaves behind grieving parents David Rollin Perry Jr. and Jennifer Marie Mauk, step-parents Tricia Perry and Andrew Mauk, and siblings Conner, Avery, Jacob, Matthew, and Danni. He was deeply loved and will be sorely missed by grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins and friends.

A funeral service will be held December 23rd at the Brookville Church of God, where beloved community member Quinton Perry’s unforgettable light will shine on.
