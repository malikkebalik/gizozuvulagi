Colleen Ballinger Wiki Biography

Colleen Ballinger was born on the 21st November 1986 in Santa Barbara, California, USA. Colleen Ballinger was raised with three siblings in Santa Barbara, by her father Tim Ballinger, who works as a sales manager, and mother Gwen, a homemaker. After graduating from San Marcos High School, she enrolled at Azusa Pacific University, to study Vocal Performance, earning a BA degree.

Her professional career began in 2007, working as a movement instructor and giving piano lessons to children, and performing at parties and cabaret spaces. In 2009, she was part of the musical “High School Musical On Stage”, as Kelsi Nielsen. Additionally, she appeared in the staged reading of “First Kids in New York” in June 2011, as Lynda Bird Johnson, and was also part of the American Theatre of Actors, playing Circe in an off-Broadway production of “Odyssey – The Epic Musical” in 2011.

Colleen launched a musical career, including collaborations with musicians such as Tim Prottey-Jones in 2010, appearing on his album “More With Every Line”, and on “Self Taught, Still Learning”, an album by Chris Passey released in 2011.

However, Colleen’s main source of income, and the main source of her fame, is her YouTube channels. She created an internet character, Miranda Sings, and a YouTube channel of the same name, and two more channels, PsychoSoprano and Colleen Vlogs. Overall, her channels have garnered more than 1.4 billion views, which has significantly increased her net worth and popularity. Thanks to her quirky and comedic character, Miranda Sings, she has been featured in a number of TV shows as a guest star, including appearances in “The Tonight Show”, and in “Comedians In Cars Getting Coffee” with Jerry Seinfeld.

Due to her successful career, Colleen has earned several prestigious awards and recognitions, including the Teen Choice Award For Web Star: Comedy in 2015, and the Streamy Awards For Performance Awards: Best Actress.

Additionally, she was named the 7th “most popular YouTube personality” by the Daily American and was also named one of the “Top 25 Digital Stars” by the Hollywood Reporter.

In terms of her personal life, she is married to Joshua David Evans, who also works as a YouTube vlogger on his channel entitled “JoshuaDTV”. They released a video about their engagement, which gained over 8.4 million views. They reside in Los Angeles.
